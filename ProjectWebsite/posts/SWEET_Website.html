SWEET Website Development

Project Date: Spring 2020

Tags

Software development

Client

SWEET Program

Summary

Designed and building a Wordpress website to promote the adaption of a framework for teamwork in class environments.
• Developed a project proposal and identified functional and nonfunctional requirements for the website.
• Communicated with the project sponsor and associates in biweekly meetings to ensure the product meets their vision

Background

WPI SWEET (Supporting WPI through Effective and Equitable Teamwork) is a grant-funded program at Worcester Polytech with the purpose of studying and promoting more effective teamwork. Worcester Polytech’s curriculum focuses deeply on project-based learning, and this program is one of the ways the school is constantly working to improve its practices. The program is run by a group of professors and students. This group put out a request for a student to build a website to promote the program.

Goals

In our first meeting the project goals were expansive and included a paywall as well as access control through WPI. However the overall goal was the promotion of the group’s research and activities to the WPI community as well as other schools.

Outcome

After the first meeting I created a project proposal which included site functional and nonfunctional requirements, the business need, potential site structure, and questions for future development. Unfortunately a key requirement was that the site was closely integrated with the WPI system, which meant that to develop the site I had to use WPI’s official software: EduBlogs. This is a fork (another, unaffiliated version) of Wordpress which is considerably more limited. As a result, the project goals had to be revised because of this technical limitation. Although with numerous conversations with WPI IT and marketing (the two departments which manage the site) I was able to open more features of the Edublogs program to meet the needs of my client.

Lessons Learned

I learned about prioritizing the needs of the client. After discovering the limitations of Edublogs, I presented a few alternative possibilities which would compromise fewer client requirements however It would mean the site did not have a wpi.edu url. This url was a top priority for the client, so I worked within the limitations of the EduBlogs system.
